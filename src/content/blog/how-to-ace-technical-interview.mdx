## Why Mock Interviews Matter

When you solve a problem on your own, you're only evaluating your technical accuracy. In a real interview, you're evaluated on:

1. **Communication:** Can you clearly explain your thought process?
2. **Collaboration:** How do you respond to hints or changing requirements?
3. **Pacing:** Are you managing your time effectively?

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

Most beginners make the same few mistakes during their first mock interview. Here's a quick code example of what *not* to do when asked a question:

```python
# Bad: Writing code silently without explaining your approach
def two_sum(nums, target):
    seen = {}
    for i, n in enumerate(nums):
        if target - n in seen:
            return [seen[target - n], i]
        seen[n] = i
```

Instead, start by outlining your approach:

1. Clarify the input and output.
2. Discuss the brute-force solution.
3. Propose an optimized approach (like using a Hash Map).
